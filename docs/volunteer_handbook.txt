VOLUNTEER HANDBOOK - HopeReach NGO

MISSION
HopeReach connects volunteers with underserved communities across education, food security, and healthcare access. Our mission is to empower communities through dedicated volunteer service and sustainable impact.

VISION
A world where every community has access to quality education, food, and healthcare through the power of volunteerism.

GETTING STARTED
1. Complete your volunteer registration form at hopereach.org/register
2. Attend the mandatory orientation session (every Saturday 10am at HopeReach HQ)
3. Choose your preferred program area
4. Complete background verification (3-5 business days)
5. Receive your volunteer badge and welcome kit
6. Join your program team on Slack

ORIENTATION
Orientation sessions are held every Saturday at 10am at HopeReach Headquarters, 123 Hope Street, Bangalore.
Duration: 2 hours
What to bring: Government ID, signed volunteer agreement form
What to expect: Program overview, team introductions, safety training, Q&A session
Virtual option: Available on request via Zoom

PROGRAM AREAS

Education Program
- Tutoring children ages 6-16 in math, science, and literacy
- Sessions: Monday, Wednesday, Friday 4pm-6pm
- Location: Community Learning Centers across Bangalore
- Skills needed: Patience, basic subject knowledge, love for teaching
- Coordinator: Priya Sharma - priya@hopereach.org

Food Security Program
- Weekly food drives and distribution
- Schedule: Every Sunday 8am-12pm at City Hall and partner locations
- Activities: Food sorting, packaging, and distribution to families in need
- Skills needed: Physical fitness, teamwork
- Coordinator: Rahul Menon - rahul@hopereach.org

Healthcare Program
- Assisting at medical camps and health awareness drives
- Schedule: Second and fourth Saturday of each month, 9am-2pm
- No medical qualification required for most roles
- Activities: Patient registration, awareness campaigns, logistics support
- Coordinator: Dr. Anita Rao - anita@hopereach.org

Environmental Program
- Tree planting, clean-up drives, and sustainability workshops
- Schedule: First Sunday of each month, 7am-11am
- Location: Various parks and public spaces in Bangalore
- Coordinator: Kiran Patel - kiran@hopereach.org

VOLUNTEER POLICIES
- Minimum commitment: 4 hours per week
- Give at least 48 hours notice if unable to attend your scheduled session
- Wear your volunteer badge at all times during activities
- Zero tolerance for discrimination, harassment, or misconduct
- Maintain confidentiality of beneficiary information
- Do not photograph beneficiaries without explicit consent
- Follow all safety guidelines provided by program coordinators
- Represent HopeReach professionally at all times

CODE OF CONDUCT
Volunteers are expected to:
- Treat all beneficiaries and fellow volunteers with dignity and respect
- Arrive on time and prepared for your volunteer session
- Report any safety concerns immediately to your coordinator
- Complete all required training before starting field work
- Not engage in any political or religious activities during volunteer hours

BENEFITS OF VOLUNTEERING
- Certificate of service after 50 hours of volunteering
- Letter of recommendation after 100 hours
- Access to HopeReach skill development workshops
- Invitation to annual volunteer appreciation dinner
- Priority consideration for HopeReach paid positions

RECOGNITION
- Volunteer of the Month award with featured profile on our website
- Annual awards for most hours, most impact, and most innovative volunteer
- Digital badges for milestone achievements (10, 25, 50, 100 hours)

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: Can I volunteer on weekends only?
A: Yes! The Food Security and Healthcare programs run on weekends. Choose the program that fits your schedule.

Q: What if I miss an orientation session?
A: Contact Sarah at sarah@hopereach.org to register for the next available session.

Q: Can I volunteer remotely?
A: Some roles in education (online tutoring) and coordination are available remotely. Ask your coordinator for details.

Q: Is there a minimum age requirement?
A: Volunteers must be at least 16 years old. Volunteers under 18 require parental consent.

Q: Will I receive training?
A: Yes, all volunteers receive program-specific training during orientation and on the job.
